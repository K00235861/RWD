Are Pandas still in danger?

By David Pilot 17/10/2019

The giant panda is a well-known animal by many people. With the panda population slowly rising, are pandas still in danger of becoming extinct?

The giant panda is part of the bear or ursine family of animals. Its natural habitat are the bamboo forests in China in which there is plenty of food for the pandas, bamboo. It makes up almost 99% of the pandas diet, which makes the panda a very inflexible animal when it comes to choosing a place to live. This very factor is the main reason of the panda being a threatened animal when it comes to extinction. Deforestation of the bamboo forests in China to make more space for people to live has forced the panda population to move, and also has significantly reduced the amount of bamboo available for consumption by pandas. Another factor worth considering is the very low birth rates of pandas. Even so, panda populations are steadily increasing.

This is due to the efforts of zoos and animal preservation organisations in China and also worldwide. Laws In China have been slowly but steadily introduced to protect pandas, which includes a ban on poaching and the removal of human settlements from dedicated panda reservation areas. This and many other factors have helped the panda population to slowly recover. In 2006, it was reported that the panda population was at roughly 3000 in the wild. In 2016, the giant panda had officially became a threatened species, a safer status endangered, which points to the conservation methods being successful. But still far from safe.

In short, if the preservation methods continue to be successful, the population of the panda will rise to safe levels.
