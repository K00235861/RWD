Butterfly Designs

By David Pilot 25/5/2019

Butterfly wings are know for their beautiful designs. But is there something more to it than just good looks?

Butterflies are insects that are present all around the world. These beautiful insects come along way from caterpillars. Once they get to this stage of their rather short lifespan, they need some kind of protection against their natural predators which include wasps, flies, small birds and other insects. They have a couple of defence mechanisms to avoid an early death. The most noticeable one being the colour and patterns of their wings.

Some butterflies use their wing colour and patterns as a form of camouflage. The oakleaf and autumn leaf butterfly have their wings look like leaves to blend in better with their surroundings. Many butterflies have eyespots on their wings, which can serve as defence, confusing an opponent tricking them to believe that they are looking at a much larger creature, or as a way to attract partners. Sometimes butterflies have exotic, bright colours on their wings, which signify something poisonous in nature, which also applies to many butterflies feeding on poisonous plants and gaining the poison as a defence. The next time you see a unique looking butterfly, now you will know that those colours and patterns are not just for show.
