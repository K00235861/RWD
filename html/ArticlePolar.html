Polar Danger

By David Pilot 1/12/2019

This is a playful image, but this may be the future for polar bears in the nest decades.

Native to the Arctic region, polar bears are adept on land as well as in the sea. These animals are in danger because of global warming, which is causing the habitat which these bears call home, the arctic circle, to decrease in size rapidly. In the late 20th century, polar bears were hunted at a large scale by hunters and poachers. Controls were put on after international concern for the polar bear population and their population recovered rather quickly. Currently, the population of the polar bear is not known to be declining or rising.

Polar bears are in a real tricky situation at this day and age. Some polar bears have been seen moving down from the arctic regions, and reports of polar bears co-existing with other species of bears in the US have been popping-up. This may happen more and more as time goes on because of global warming.
