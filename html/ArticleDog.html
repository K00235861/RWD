Dogs vs Wolves

By Dave Potato Pilot 1/11/2019

Ever look at a dog and think “How different are dogs and wolves?” No? Well, this article will answer that question anyway!

Even though dogs and wolves are genetically similar, there are many differences to be found between man’s best friend and a fearsome wolf. Interestingly enough, a dogs head and brain are 20% and 30% smaller respectively compared to a similar sized wolf. Dogs have a distinctive “stop” between forehead and nose compared to wolves. A wolves bite is much more powerful compared to a dogs. This is because the muscles used to close the jaw, the temporalis muscle, is much stronger on a wolf. Additionally, wolfs do not have a dewclaw on their back legs, which is a small distal part (almost like a finger or toe) of a many animal limbs. A wolfs paw is on average twice as big as a dogs and have amber or light coloured eyes compared to the usual brown dog eyes. Another difference is that a dogs tail tends to curl upwards, something that is not found on wolves.

These changes happened over tens of thousands of years. So the next time you have a look at a dog, you can think about how much they have changed from their wolf ancestors.
